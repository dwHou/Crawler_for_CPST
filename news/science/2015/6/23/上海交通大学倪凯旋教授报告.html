Seminar Talk at CTP-SCU / 理论物理中心学术报告

Speaker: Prof. Kai-Xuan Ni （倪凯旋教授） (Shanghai Jiao Tong U. 上海交通大学)

Title: Status and Challenges of Dark Matter Direct Detection

Abstract:

Uncovering the particle nature of dark matter is one of the most challenging problems in physics today. By using low background underground detectors, dark matter may be detected via their collision with the atoms. Experimental sensitivity has been improved by more than one order of magnitude in the last decade. However, we still don’t have a clear signal from dark matter. In this talk, I will review the most advanced detection technology and the current experimental progress on the direct detection of dark matter. I will also discuss future challenges.

Time: 6/25 (Thu) 4:00pm

Venue: Physics building 323, CTP-SCU
