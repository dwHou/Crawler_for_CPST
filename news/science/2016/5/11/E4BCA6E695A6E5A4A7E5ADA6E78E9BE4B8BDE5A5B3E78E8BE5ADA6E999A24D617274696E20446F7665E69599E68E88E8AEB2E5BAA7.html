题目：The story of the neutron

时间：5月16日（周一）、5月18日（周三）晚上7-9点
地点：江安校区综合楼C座C406
讲座摘要：
The neutron was the final atomic particle to be discovered (Predicted by Ernest Rutherford in 1920, discovered by James Chadwick in 1932), but in just a short period of time the knowledge of the neutron transformed the world. In these lectures I will discuss the story of the neutron and some of its applications in science. I will highlight the fact that China is building a new powerful source of neutron beams for research into the physics and chemistry of materials.

Martin Dove教授的简介：
School of Physics and Astronomy, Queen Mary University of London, London, United Kingdom
Prof Martin Dove is Director of the Centre for Condensed Matter and Materials Physics in Queen Mary University of London. His research areas include the use of neutron beams for research into the atomic structure and dynamics of materials, coupled with computer simulation. He obtained his PhD in the University of Birmingham, and was previously a professor in the University of Cambridge. He will be giving two courses of lectures in the forthcoming University Immersion Programme.
