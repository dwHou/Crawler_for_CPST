报告题目： Raman Scattering-Photoluminescence-Synchrotron Combined Studies on S iC Bulk and Epitaxial Structures
报告摘要：
SiC is now recognizing as the 21 st century power electronic materials, which is more attractive and significant for the world range energy-saving technology and economy.
The author has worked on SiC since 1983. This presentation reviews the recent important results from the author’s SiC research team at NTU, employing multiple techniques of high-resolution (HR) & short wavelength (SW) X-ray diffraction (XRD) , high-resolution transmission electron microscopy (HRTEM), Raman scattering (RS) , photoluminescence (PL) , photoluminescence excitation (PLE), t ime resolved (TR) PL , Fourier transform infrared (FTIR), second harmonic generation (SHG), femtosecond pulsed laser spectroscopy, X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS), secondary ion mass spectroscopy (SIMS), Rutherford backscattering (RBS) and ion channeling, Synchrotron radiation (SR) high energy XRD and X-ray absorption (XAS) and so on.
Our wide range of interdisciplinary studies on these and related materials structures have explored and revealed many interesting phenomena. Some puzzling and unsolved problems are raised. Some theoretical calculations and further needs are introduced and discussed. It is also important to stimulate further investigation of SiC for other possible optoelectronic applications besides the current major focus of SiC research for high speed and high power devices.
报告人简介：
Prof. Zhe Chuan FENG, received the BS (1968) and M.S. (1981) from Peking University, and Ph. D in University of Pittsburgh, 1987; worked at Emory University (1988-92), National University of Singapore (92-94), Georgia Tech (95 , 2002-03), EMCORE Corporation (95-97), Institute of Materials Research & Engineering, Singapore (98-2001), and Axcel Photonics (2001-02), in all places with fruitful results and achievements. Since August 2003, Feng has joined National Taiwan University as a professor at Institute of Photonics and Optoelectronics & Department of Electrical Engineering, currently focusing on materials investigation (especially Raman/PL, surface science & synchrotron technology) and growth of wide gap semiconductors, III-Nitrides, SiC, ZnO, and III-V, II-VI, oxides and other nano-materials/devices. He has published nine review books on advanced compound semiconductors and microstructures, porous Si, SiC, III-Nitrides, ZnO. Feng has published >530 scientific papers with >220 recorded by Science Citation Index (SCI) and cited >2500 times. Feng is a member of international organizing committee of Asia CVD, a board member of Taiwan Association for Thin Films & Coating Technology, Guest professors at Nankai University, Tianjin Normal University, Huazhong University of Science & Technology, South China Normal University ,Sichuan University and Nanjing Tech University. He has been awarded as the 2013 SPIE Fellow.
