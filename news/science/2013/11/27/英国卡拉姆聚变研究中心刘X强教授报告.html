题目：
Plasma response, flow damping and RMP penetration
报告人：
Prof. Yueqiang Liu
Euratom/CCFE Fusion Association, Culham Science Centre, Abingdon, OX14 3DB, UK
报告人简介：
He worked at Chalmers University of Technology, Sweden from 1999 till 2008, holding various positions: post-doc in 1999; assistant professor during 2000-2003; associate professor during 2003-2008. He is also presently an adjunct professor at Chalmers. Since 2008, Dr. Liu has been working at CCFE, as a Senior Fusion Theorist.
Dr. Liu has so far authored/co-authored over 100 peer-reviewed journal papers, 32 invited talks at international conferences and other 150 conference contributions. His Hirsch index was 23 in October 2012, according to the Web of Science.
Research interests
Resistive wall mode
Active control
MHD and drift kinetic theory
ELM mitigation using RMP fields
Plasma edge physics
Numerical modelling of macroscopic instabilities in fusion plasmas
Current research
Physics and control of resistive wall mode
Plasma response to resonant magnetic perturbation fields
Error field induced plasma rotation damping
3D electromagnetic effects on macroscopic instability and response of fusion plasmas
Dr. Liu is currently working on theory and modelling of macroscopic instabilities in fusion plasmas. In particular, he has been working on magnetic feedback and kinetic stabilisation of the so-called resistive wall modes, that can pose severe limitation on the advanced fusion plasma scenarios, such as those foreseen for ITER.
